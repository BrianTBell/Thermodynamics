Thermodynamics

What is Thermodynamics?

Thermodynamics is the study of energy, heat, and work interactions within physical systems. It explores how energy transforms between forms, governing processes in engines, refrigerators, and power plants. Key concepts include temperature, pressure, entropy, and enthalpy, which define system behavior during processes like heating, compression, or expansion. Thermodynamics underpins engineering designs, ensuring efficiency and performance in systems from HVAC to jet engines.

1. Fundamental Properties

Pressure: \( P = \frac{F}{A} \)

Temperature (ideal gas): \( T = \frac{P V}{n R} \)

Density: \( \rho = \frac{m}{V} \)

Specific Volume: \( v = \frac{V}{m} = \frac{1}{\rho} \)

Ideal Gas Law: \( P V = n R T \) or \( P v = R T \) (per unit mass)

Gas Constant: \( R = \frac{\bar{R}}{M} \), \( \bar{R} = 8.314 \, \text{J/(mol·K)} \)

2. First Law of Thermodynamics

Closed System: \( \Delta U = Q - W \)

Open System (Steady Flow): \( \dot{Q} - \dot{W} = \dot{m} \left[ \left( h_2 - h_1 \right) + \frac{1}{2} \left( v_2^2 - v_1^2 \right) + g \left( z_2 - z_1 \right) \right] \)

Internal Energy: \( \Delta U = m c_v \Delta T \) (constant volume)

Enthalpy: \( h = u + P v \), \( \Delta H = m c_p \Delta T \) (constant pressure)

Work (Closed System): \( W = \int P \, dV \)

3. Second Law of Thermodynamics

Entropy Change: \( \Delta S = \int \frac{dQ_{\text{rev}}}{T} \)

Ideal Gas Entropy: \( \Delta s = c_p \ln \frac{T_2}{T_1} - R \ln \frac{P_2}{P_1} \) or \( \Delta s = c_v \ln \frac{T_2}{T_1} + R \ln \frac{v_2}{v_1} \)

Carnot Efficiency: \( \eta = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H} \) (absolute temperatures)

Clausius Inequality: \( \oint \frac{dQ}{T} \leq 0 \)

4. Thermodynamic Processes

Isothermal (\( T = \text{constant} \)):

Work: \( W = P_1 V_1 \ln \frac{V_2}{V_1} = n R T \ln \frac{V_2}{V_1} \)

Isobaric (\( P = \text{constant} \)):

Work: \( W = P \Delta V \)

Isochoric (\( V = \text{constant} \)):

Work: \( W = 0 \)

Adiabatic (\( Q = 0 \)):

\( P V^\gamma = \text{constant} \), \( T V^{\gamma-1} = \text{constant} \), \( \gamma = \frac{c_p}{c_v} \)

Work: \( W = \frac{P_2 V_2 - P_1 V_1}{1 - \gamma} \)

5. Thermodynamic Cycles

Carnot Cycle Efficiency: \( \eta = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H} \)

Rankine Cycle Efficiency: \( \eta = \frac{W_{\text{turbine}} - W_{\text{pump}}}{Q_{\text{in}}} \)

Otto Cycle Efficiency: \( \eta = 1 - \frac{1}{r^{\gamma-1}} \), \( r = \frac{V_{\text{max}}}{V_{\text{min}}} \)

Brayton Cycle Efficiency: \( \eta = 1 - \frac{1}{r_p^{(\gamma-1)/\gamma}} \), \( r_p = \frac{P_{\text{max}}}{P_{\text{min}}} \)

6. Heat Transfer

Conduction (Fourier’s Law): \( q = -k A \frac{dT}{dx} \)

Convection: \( q = h A (T_s - T_\infty) \)

Radiation: \( q = \epsilon \sigma A T^4 \), \( \sigma = 5.67 \times 10^{-8} \, \text{W/(m}^2\text{·K}^4\text{)} \)

Overall Heat Transfer: \( Q = U A \Delta T \), \( U = \text{overall heat transfer coefficient} \)

7. Properties of Pure Substances

Saturated Liquid-Vapor: Use steam tables for \( P, T, v, u, h, s \).

Quality (vapor mass fraction): \( x = \frac{m_{\text{vapor}}}{m_{\text{total}}} \)

Specific Property: \( y = y_f + x (y_g - y_f) \), \( y = v, u, h, s \)

Compressibility Factor: \( Z = \frac{P v}{R T} \), \( Z = 1 \) for ideal gas.

8. Gas Mixtures

Dalton’s Law: \( P = \sum P_i \), \( P_i = y_i P \) (partial pressure)

Amagat’s Law: \( V = \sum V_i \), \( V_i = y_i V \) (partial volume)

Mole Fraction: \( y_i = \frac{n_i}{n} \)

Mass Fraction: \( m_i = \frac{m_i}{m} \)
